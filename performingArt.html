objectives are

• To understand the market (Domestic and global) – what are the challenges/ gaps, best practices, and scope

• To explore the current status of customer – the needs, how is it met (suppliers), process etc and what are their future expectation (demand, nature of agencies etc)

• To find out the suppliers – artists as what are their expertise, how are the managing their business at present, challenges, future plan and support required

• To find out the suppliers / facilitators (event management companies) as what are their expertise, how are they engaging with artists as well as customers, how are getting business, challenges, future plan

• To propose business model – business/ services/ products, potential customers and suppliers, financial projection etc

The findings of from the recent study conducted by ekalakaar.com

• The world economy is set to surpass $100 trillion. Global media market is expected to grow from $1712.96 billion in 2020 to $1850.04 billion in 2021 at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8%. The independent artists and performing art companies expected to reach $181.39 billion in 2021 at a CAGR of 16.2%, twice of media market. The Indian media market is projected to reach $39 bn by 2024 at a CAGR of 9% and performing art is expected to reach by $3.7 bn.

• Global Digital Content Creation Market size is estimated to be US$ 11 billion in 2019 and is expected to reach US$ 38.2 billion by 2030 at a CAGR of 12%, which is 1.5 times of media market. Indian Digital content market size is expected to reach by $1.6 bn.

• Global Digital Content Creation Market size is estimated to be US$ 11 billion in 2019 and is expected to reach US$ 38.2 billion by 2030 at a CAGR of 12%, which is 1.5 times of media market. Indian Digital content market size is expected to reach by $1.6 bn.

• M&E industry is growing but struggling for suitable content for its customers as evident with higher growth of digital content (12% against overall media of 8%). Other hand, the traditional / performing art segment and budding artists have huge and diversified multilingual content.

• Considering the grow of the media sector and hunger for audience friendly content in one hand, and the huge available content in traditional performing art sector, there is need of present the traditional performing art for modern diverse customers. The sector seems to be unorganized, as there is mismatch between the customers/ demand and suppliers/ artists. Event management companies might be facilitating between two in local level but they may also lack standards and access to both demand and supply sides.

• A quick dipstick was done to get the perspective of the customers (youth) on entertainment and traditional performing art. The study includes 106 artists across the country, 15 film makers and customers as well as secondary research. Traditional performing art is watched by over 60% at least weekly and 80% of artists want to watch in original form, and 75% also open for fusion. They are keen to watch the such art as 40% are enthusiasts, 35% are nostalgia, remaining use it for education and spiritual purpose.
